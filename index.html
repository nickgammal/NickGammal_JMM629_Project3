What is a Wandering Albatross?

What are they like?

Hello! This is a wandering albatross. They are large seabirds that spend the majority of their life "wandering" about the Southern Ocean. Albatrosses are the group of birds with the largest wingspan in the world. Enter your height in centimeters to the figure below to see how tall you are compared to their wingspan. Despite their great size, these birds typically only weigh about 25 kilograms, surprisingly light for such a large animal!























































Where do they live?

Wandering albatrosses spend most of their time flying or floating on the ocean. They spend very little time on land, and when they do it is only in coastal or island regions of the antarctic and subantarctic. Notice the lack of data in the South Pacific Ocean. This reflects the observation bias due to increased scientific effort in waters near Australia and near scientific stations on the Antarctic Peninsula.




















































How often do they land?

Wandering albatrosses can fly for about 16,000 kilometers without resting on land or on water. They only spend time regularly on land every two years during breeding season (which lasts from the end of November to the beginning of March). These two maps show the locations of albatrosses seen during breeding season (Blue) and outside of breeding season (Yellow). Notice that a large proportion of the birds seen during mating season were seen over open ocean. That is beacuse only about half the birds will breed each season while the other half is "on sabbatical". This phenomenon is called "temporal reproductive isolation," which can lead to the two breeding groups evolving into separate species over generational timescales.
